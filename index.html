Buffett's Top 3 Rules

1. Margin of Safety

"Price is what you pay; value is what you get." Buy great businesses at a discount to intrinsic value.

2. Economic Moats

"In business, I look for economic castles protected by unbreachable moats." Seek durable competitive advantages.

3. Long-Term Thinking

"Our favorite holding period is forever." Let compounding work over decades, not days.

Howard Marks on Price vs. Value

"The relationship between price and value holds the ultimate key to investment success."
— Howard Marks
  • There is no asset so good it can't become overpriced
  • There is no asset so bad it can't become a bargain
  • The biggest risk is paying too much, even for a great asset
  • Second-level thinking separates great investors from average ones
